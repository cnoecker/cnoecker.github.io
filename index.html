About me

Hi! I am a NRSA postdoctoral fellow in the lab of Peter Turnbaugh at UC San Francisco. Broadly, my research seeks to decipher the metabolism, ecology, and evolution of mammalian gut microbes, especially those with unconventional metabolic lifestyles. I use experimental and computational tools in microbiology, genomics, metabolomics, and systems biology. I am also a dedicated teacher and mentor, with a focus on reducing educational barriers in computational biology. I aim to obtain a faculty position at a primarily undergraduate institution (PUI), where I can teach microbiology and bioinformatics and work with students to continue to unravel the workings of gut microbes. You can find a copy of my CV here.

My training and previous research projects spanned bioinformatics, genetics, virology, and applied statistics.

My last name is pronounced Nay-ker (rhymes with Baker).

I grew up in St. Paul, Minnesota. Outside of science, I enjoy cooking, running, reading, and quality time with family and friends.
